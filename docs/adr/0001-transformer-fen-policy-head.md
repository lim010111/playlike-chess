# Transformer over FEN tokens with policy head over fixed action space

We chose a Transformer that takes a single FEN string as tokenized text input and outputs logits over a fixed action space (~1968 UCI move encodings), rather than an LLM-style decoder over move-sequence tokens (α) or an AlphaZero-style ResNet over an 8×8×channels board tensor (β). This keeps the board state explicit (avoiding the implicit-board, long-context problem of (α)), makes illegal-move masking a one-line operation at the policy head, and aligns with LLM tooling for LoRA-based per-player adapters — strengths the other two options each lack.

DeepMind's 2024 "Grandmaster-Level Chess Without Search" (Ruoss et al.) demonstrates the approach is viable at scales that fit the 3080 10GB compute budget. The paper reports Tournament Elo 2007 (±15) at 9M parameters — but for the *action-value* (AV) prediction target trained on Stockfish-annotated boards. Our project trains *behavioral cloning* (BC) on human played-moves, which the paper's Table 2 shows is materially weaker at the same scale (65.7% vs 83.3% puzzle accuracy at 9M; the paper does not report a Tournament Elo for 9M-BC). Realistic Base Elo expectation is therefore closer to the training-data distribution itself (~1800 Lichess blitz, since we filter to ≥1800-rated games) than to the 2007 AV figure. This still meets the (C)-grade target (1500–1800 minimum).

Per-Player Adapter strength relative to Base is treated as **empirical, not assumed**. Two competing forces are at play: (a) Adapter training data is materially higher-quality than Base data (Super-GM blitz vs ~1800-rated blitz), which could lift Adapter strength above Base; (b) v1's LoRA scope is rank-16 on attention QKVO only — FFN, embedding, and policy head are frozen — which constrains the Adapter to attention-routing reweights and prevents it from absorbing genuinely new chess knowledge that the 9M Base does not already represent. Either direction is plausible at v1: Adapter may modestly exceed, match, or trail Base. The evaluation harness treats >100 Elo regression as a fail signal that triggers v2 LoRA-scope expansion (see PRD Out of Scope); anything within ±100 Elo of Base is treated as success on the strength axis, with stylistic divergence carried by opening repertoire KL.

BC over AV is a deliberate choice: AV would yield a stronger model at the same scale but collapse policy toward Stockfish's universal "best move" — the exact failure mode the problem statement names ("Stockfish plays objectively strong chess, not anyone's chess"). Strength ceiling at 9M-BC is the cost; preserving the human-variability substrate that per-Player Adapters specialize on is the benefit.

## Markov input — explicit trade-off

The input is a single FEN per request: no move history, no prior board states. Accepted because (a) at 9M params / 10GB VRAM the model cannot meaningfully learn long-horizon plans even if history were available, (b) adding history would multiply sequence length ~6–8× and force the model to shrink, and (c) per-Player archives are small (~10K Games), so same-FEN generalization across Games matters more than path-conditioning.

Known costs accepted for v1: path-conditioned style is not learnable — gambit follow-through, transposition-aware play, plan continuity from earlier in the Game. Threefold-repetition awareness must be added at the server's legality layer rather than learned by the model. AlphaZero feeds T=8 prior board-state planes precisely for these reasons; we are deliberately not following that pattern.

Revisit trigger: if v1 evaluation or qualitative review shows clear path-conditioning deficits (transposition-zone errors, abandoned gambit themes), introduce a small history window (e.g. T=4 prior board states) and shrink the model to fit. Until then, path-dependence is out of scope.

Note: the temperature-sampling decision (Q13) provides *variation* across replays of the same opening; it does not provide *path-conditioning* and is not a substitute for history. The two are orthogonal axes.
