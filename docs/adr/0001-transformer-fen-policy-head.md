# Transformer over FEN tokens with policy head over fixed action space

We chose a Transformer that takes a single FEN string as tokenized text input and outputs logits over a fixed action space (~1968 UCI move encodings), rather than an LLM-style decoder over move-sequence tokens (α) or an AlphaZero-style ResNet over an 8×8×channels board tensor (β). This keeps the board state explicit (avoiding the implicit-board, long-context problem of (α)), makes illegal-move masking a one-line operation at the policy head, and aligns with LLM tooling for LoRA-based per-player adapters — strengths the other two options each lack.

DeepMind's 2024 "Grandmaster-Level Chess Without Search" (Ruoss et al.) demonstrates the approach is viable at scales that fit the 3080 10GB compute budget. The paper reports Tournament Elo 2007 (±15) at 9M parameters — but for the *action-value* (AV) prediction target trained on Stockfish-annotated boards. Our project trains *behavioral cloning* (BC) on human played-moves, which the paper's Table 2 shows is materially weaker at the same scale (65.7% vs 83.3% puzzle accuracy at 9M; the paper does not report a Tournament Elo for 9M-BC). Realistic Base Elo expectation is therefore closer to the training-data distribution itself (~1800 Lichess blitz, since we filter to ≥1800-rated games) than to the 2007 AV figure. This still meets the (C)-grade target (1500–1800 minimum).

Per-Player Adapter strength relative to Base is treated as **empirical, not assumed**. Two competing forces are at play: (a) Adapter training data is materially higher-quality than Base data (Super-GM blitz vs ~1800-rated blitz), which could lift Adapter strength above Base; (b) v1's LoRA scope is rank-16 on attention QKVO only — FFN, embedding, and policy head are frozen — which constrains the Adapter to attention-routing reweights and prevents it from absorbing genuinely new chess knowledge that the 9M Base does not already represent. Either direction is plausible at v1: Adapter may modestly exceed, match, or trail Base.

The strength comparison is measured by **direct head-to-head matches between Base and Base+Adapter** (100 games, blitz TC, alternating colors, sample decoding) — not by reading off external-pool ratings, since Elo is fundamentally a relative measure and the ~300-Elo-bucket Fairy-Stockfish ladder cannot resolve a 100-Elo gap. Adapter score percentage is converted to an Elo delta via the standard formula. >100 Elo regression (Adapter score < 36%) is the fail signal that triggers v2 LoRA-scope expansion (see PRD Out of Scope); anything within ±100 Elo of Base is treated as success on the strength axis, with stylistic divergence carried by opening repertoire KL.

BC over AV is a deliberate choice: AV would yield a stronger model at the same scale but collapse policy toward Stockfish's universal "best move" — the exact failure mode the problem statement names ("Stockfish plays objectively strong chess, not anyone's chess"). Strength ceiling at 9M-BC is the cost; preserving the human-variability substrate that per-Player Adapters specialize on is the benefit.

## Markov input — explicit trade-off

The input is a single FEN per request: no move history, no prior board states. Accepted because (a) at 9M params / 10GB VRAM the model cannot meaningfully learn long-horizon plans even if history were available, (b) adding history would multiply sequence length ~6–8× and force the model to shrink, and (c) per-Player archives are small (~10K Games), so same-FEN generalization across Games matters more than path-conditioning.

Known costs accepted for v1: path-conditioned style is not learnable — gambit follow-through, transposition-aware play, plan continuity from earlier in the Game. Threefold-repetition awareness must be added at the server's legality layer rather than learned by the model. AlphaZero feeds T=8 prior board-state planes precisely for these reasons; we are deliberately not following that pattern.

Revisit trigger: if v1 evaluation or qualitative review shows clear path-conditioning deficits (transposition-zone errors, abandoned gambit themes), introduce a small history window (e.g. T=4 prior board states) and shrink the model to fit. Until then, path-dependence is out of scope.

Note: the temperature-sampling decision (Q13) provides *variation* across replays of the same opening; it does not provide *path-conditioning* and is not a substitute for history. The two are orthogonal axes.

## Sub-architecture — deferred to issue 03 with mandatory survey

Within the macro decision above (Transformer over canonical FEN tokens, policy head over 1968 UCI actions, ~9M parameters), the *sub-architectural* details are deliberately left open at the foundation level: layer count, hidden dim, head count, positional-encoding scheme (learned absolute vs sinusoidal vs RoPE vs ALiBi vs 2D-board-aware), LayerNorm placement (pre-LN vs post-LN), MLP activation (GELU vs SwiGLU vs ReLU²), attention kernel (vanilla MHA vs `F.scaled_dot_product_attention` vs FlashAttention 2), mixed-precision dtype, dropout, init, tied embeddings. None of these leak past the model module — the external contract is just `(int tensor [B, ~80]) → logits [B, 1968]` — so deferring costs nothing downstream and decisions made after seeing real corpus statistics and 3080 forward-pass measurements are strictly better-informed than ones made at foundation time.

**Encoder-only with a single readout vector → linear projection** is locked even though the rest is deferred: it bounds the forward graph (no causal mask over the FEN tokens, no autoregressive decoding loop) so downstream modules (Inference Engine, legality mask application, decoding modes) can be specified concretely now without waiting on sub-architecture decisions.

The baseline starting point is Ruoss et al.'s 9M configuration (DeepMind 2024). However, **deviation from that baseline requires an explicit pre-training architecture survey** — vanilla post-LN encoder transformer is no longer the 2026 default for small-scale models, and the gap between "DeepMind paper as-is" and the current small-transformer chassis (pre-LN, RoPE or learned absolute, SwiGLU/GLU MLPs, FlashAttention 2, bf16 mixed precision, possibly 2D board-aware positional structure) is large enough that locking the paper's exact shape without surveying 2024–2026 literature would leave free performance on the table. The survey is mandated as an acceptance criterion of issue 03 (real Base training), not deferred to v2 — the per-axis decisions and their rationale must be written down before any 9M training run is started, so that future ablation has a clean baseline and any strength miss has a small enough search space to diagnose.

Issue 02 (toy ML tracer at ~1M params) is exempt — it deliberately uses a placeholder architecture; the survey is gated only at issue 03 when real strength matters.
